
### 1. Overview

Organized cybercrime refers to **well-structured criminal groups** that operate in the digital world for **financial gain**. These groups resemble traditional mafias but conduct their operations **online**, exploiting the **anonymity and global reach** of the internet.

### 2. Structure and Operations

- Operate like traditional organized crime groups with **defined roles** (leaders, hackers, money launderers, etc.).
- Use **advanced hacking techniques** such as custom malware, ransomware, and phishing.
- Often function as **transnational networks**, making prosecution difficult for law enforcement.
- Highly **coordinated and strategic** in planning and executing attacks.

### 3. Technical Capabilities

- Use tools like **malware, phishing kits, and ransomware**.
- Employ **cryptocurrencies** for untraceable transactions.
- Leverage the **dark web** for illegal trade and recruitment.
- Utilize **emerging technologies** and constantly adapt to new security defenses.

### 4. Motivation

- **Primary goal:** Financial profit.
- Conduct activities like **data breaches, identity theft, online fraud**, and **ransomware attacks**.
- Target **SMEs**, **banks**, and **wealthy individuals** with valuable data.
- Occasionally act as **mercenaries for governments** or political entities for pay.

### 5. Notable Examples

- **FIN7:**
    - Known for sophisticated phishing campaigns targeting retail and hospitality sectors.
    - Stole sensitive data via fraudulent emails and malware.
- **Carbanak Group:**

    - Stole over **$1 billion** from global banks.
    - Used the **Carbanak malware** to infiltrate banking systems and remotely control ATMs.


### 6. Key Takeaway

Organized cybercrime groups combine **traditional criminal hierarchy** with **advanced technical expertise**, making them one of the **most dangerous and profitable** threat actors in modern cyberspace.
