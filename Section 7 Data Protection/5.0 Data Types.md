
### **Data Types

#### **1. Core Concept: Not All Data is Equal**

Different types of data have different values, sensitivities, and legal requirements. Understanding these categories is essential for applying the correct level of protection and ensuring compliance.

---

#### **2. Key Data Types & Their Protections**

| Data Type                                     | Description                                                                  | Examples                                                                                            | Key Regulations / Protections                                                                       |
| :-------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Regulated Data**                            | Information controlled by laws, regulations, or industry standards.          | PII (Personal Identification Information), PHI (Protected Health Information), Credit Card Data<br> | GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS                                                                                |
| **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)** | Any data that can be used to identify a specific individual.                 | Name, SSN, Address, Driver's License                                                                | Privacy laws (e.g., GDPR, CCPA)                                                                     |
| **PHI (Protected Health Information)**        | Health information that can be linked to an individual.                      | Medical records, diagnoses, billing info, payment history                                           | **HIPAA** (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)                                     |
| **Trade Secrets**                             | Confidential business information that provides a competitive advantage.     | Manufacturing processes, proprietary formulas, customer lists, marketing strategies                 | Protected by law (e.g., Economic Espionage Act); unauthorized disclosure leads to severe penalties. |
| **Intellectual Property (IP)**                | Creations of the mind, inventions, and artistic works.                       | Software code, course videos, patents, copyrights, trademarks                                       | Protected by **patents, copyrights, and trademarks**. Unauthorized use can lead to legal action.    |
| **Legal Information**                         | Data related to legal proceedings, contracts, or compliance.                 | Client case files, contracts, legal opinions                                                        | Protected by attorney-client privilege; requires high confidentiality.                              |
| **Financial Information**                     | Data related to an organization's or individual's financial transactions.    | Sales records, tax documents, invoices, bank statements                                             | **PCI DSS** (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard); targeted for fraud and identity theft.  |
| **Human-Readable Data**                       | Information that can be understood directly by people without special tools. | Text documents, spreadsheets, PDFs                                                                  | -                                                                                                   |
| **Non-Human Readable Data**                   | Data that requires a machine or software to be interpreted.                  | Binary files, database files, machine code, encrypted data                                          | Still contains sensitive information and must be protected.                                         |

---

### **Key Takeaways & Mnemonics**

*   **Regulation Focus:**
    *   **Health Data?** Think **HIPAA**.
    *   **Personal Data in EU?** Think **GDPR**.
    *   **Credit Card Data?** Think **PCI DSS**.

*   **PII vs. PHI:** All PHI is PII, but not all PII is PHI. PHI is a specific subset of PII related to health.

*   **Business-Critical Data:**
    *   **Trade Secrets:** The "secret sauce" that gives a competitive edge (e.g., the method for creating a popular course).
    *   **Intellectual Property (IP):** The creative output itself (e.g., the actual course videos).

*   **All Data Must Be Protected:** Even **non-human readable** data (like binary code) can contain sensitive information and is not inherently secure.

*   **Why This Matters:** Correctly classifying data allows an organization to:
    *   Protect valuable assets.
    *   Comply with laws and avoid penalties.
    *   Maintain customer trust and organizational reputation.
